Andrey Tsepkov:

I was born in 1978 in Siberian settlement of wood-treating factory. After higher school, I went to Omsk State Technical University in 1995. And then, in 2000 I got a diploma of industry engineer in polygraphy.

I used to work as a polygraphist for years and then realized that I have no motivation to grow as a professional. To print books and newspapers is not the same as to read them — and I loved to read a lot. After a while, I remembered that there were funny lessons of programming in Basic in school. Also, those incredibly exciting cryptography tricks — ciphers, secret codes, matrixes, etc. So I decided to become a programmist.

I started with HTML/CSS and Javascript — after shallow learning of Python’s basics. I worked out on Node.js project of the site for a traveling agency. After some time, there was a remarkable meeting with C#, .NET and Visual Studio. So, now it’s a compelling immersion into ASP.NET MVC, ASP.NET Core and building a single page application with WebAPI and Vue.js for frontend.

I want to be a professional in backend programming and I learn a lot. I write code and look for a real job in web-development. I need to get complete and thorough experience with popular technologies. So, if you have something interesting to offer, just tell me. :)
