Andrey Tsepkov:

I was born in 1978 in Siberian settlement of wood-treating factory. After higher school, I went to Omsk State Technical University in 1995. And then, in 2000 I got a diploma of industry engineer in polygraphy.

I used to work as a polygraphist for years and then realized that I have no motivation to grow as a professional. To print books and newspapers is not the same as to read them — and I loved to read a lot. After a while, I remembered that there were funny lessons of programming in Basic in school. Also, those incredibly exciting cryptography tricks — ciphers, secret codes, matrixes, etc. So I decided to become a programmist.

I started with HTML/CSS and Javascript — after shallow learning of Python’s basics. I worked out on Node.js project of the site for a traveling agency. After some time, there was a remarkable meeting with C#, .NET and Visual Studio. So, now it’s a compelling immersion into ASP.NET MVC, ASP.NET Core and building a single page application with WebAPI and Typescript for frontend.

I want to be a professional in backend programming. It would be nice to know tools like Haskell and Ruby too, also to stabilize the relationship with Python. I appreciate the necessitate of continuous growing and self-development to be a valuable expert. And I love to learn. And to write a good working code.
